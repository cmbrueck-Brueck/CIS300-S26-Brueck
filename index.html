Understanding the Basics

Fiction

Fiction refers to literature created from the imagination. These are stories featuring made-up characters, settings, and plots. While they can be inspired by real events or tackle real-world themes, the narrative itself is invented by the author.

Popular Examples:

Fantasy Romance Mystery Sci-Fi Thrillers

Non-Fiction

Non-fiction is grounded entirely in reality. These books are based on facts, actual events, and real people. The primary goal of a non-fiction book is to inform, educate, or document the truth about the world around us.

Popular Examples:

Biographies History Self-Help Memoirs True Crime
