Understanding the Basics

If you are new to the reading community, the terminology can sometimes feel like a completely different language. To get you started on the right foot, here is a quick breakdown of the two main umbrellas that every single book falls under.

Fiction

Fiction refers to literature created from the imagination. These are stories featuring made-up characters, settings, and plots. While they can be inspired by real events, tackle real-world themes, or even take place in real cities, the narrative itself is invented by the author to entertain, thrill, or transport you.

Popular Examples:

  • ●Fantasy
  • ●Romance
  • ●Mystery & Thrillers
  • ●Sci-Fi

Non-Fiction

Non-fiction is grounded entirely in reality. These books are based on facts, actual events, and real people. The primary goal of a non-fiction book is to inform, educate, or document the truth about the world around us. Reading these is a fantastic way to walk in someone else's shoes.

Popular Examples:

  • ●Biographies
  • ●History
  • ●Self-Help
  • ●Memoirs
