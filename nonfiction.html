Real Life, Real Stories: Non-Fiction

They often say that truth is stranger than fiction, and the non-fiction genre proves that point time and time again. While getting lost in a made-up world is a wonderful escape, there is something deeply grounding and profoundly inspiring about reading stories that actually happened. These books expand our understanding of the universe, our history, and ourselves.

Reading non-fiction is one of the most accessible ways to travel through time, step into someone else’s shoes, or sit down with world-class experts to learn a new skill. Whether you are looking to be entertained by a scandalous historical event, moved to tears by an incredible memoir, or motivated to change your daily habits, this genre has the power to literally change your life.

Because non-fiction encompasses everything under the sun, it can sometimes feel overwhelming to know where to start. To help you navigate, I have broken down my top recommendations into three core subgenres below. Get ready to learn something new!

Biographies & Memoirs

There is no better way to understand the human experience than by stepping directly into someone else's memories. Biographies and memoirs allow us to witness history through the eyes of the people who actually lived it. From world leaders and scientific geniuses to ordinary people who survived extraordinary circumstances, these books are deeply intimate and relentlessly fascinating.

While a biography is a sweeping look at a person's entire life (often written by an historian or journalist), a memoir is written by the subject themselves and usually focuses on a specific, defining era of their journey. Both formats offer an unparalleled level of empathy, vulnerability, and jaw-dropping true stories that will stick with you forever.

NYT Best Seller

Becoming

Michelle Obama

Former First Lady Michelle Obama invites readers into her world, chronicling the experiences that have shaped her from her childhood on the South Side of Chicago to her years at the White House. With unerring honesty and lively wit, she describes her triumphs and her disappointments, both public and private. It is a deeply personal, warmly inspiring memoir of a woman of soul and substance.

My Recommendation

The Diary of a Young Girl

Anne Frank

Discovered in the attic where she spent the last years of her life, this remarkable diary offers an intimate, heartbreaking glimpse into the mind of a teenage girl hiding from the Nazis. Anne's voice is vibrant, humorous, and remarkably perceptive, capturing the daily struggles and fleeting joys of a life in confinement. It is an essential, profound piece of history that continues to resonate with readers around the world.

Get the Book
If you liked the movie 'Oppenheimer'...

American Prometheus

Kai Bird & Martin J. Sherwin

This is the Pulitzer Prize-winning, definitive biography of J. Robert Oppenheimer that inspired the massive cinematic blockbuster. It is a thrilling, meticulously researched exploration of genius, ambition, and the devastating moral consequences of creating the atomic bomb. It provides an incredibly rich, complex portrait of a man who changed the course of human history.

History Books

If you think history books are just dry lists of dates and battles, you are reading the wrong ones! Narrative history takes the real events of our past and structures them with the pacing, tension, and character development of a brilliant thriller novel. These books prove that the drama of the real world often outshines anything a fiction author could invent.

Exploring history helps us understand exactly how our modern world was built. Whether you are reading about the sudden collapse of an ancient empire, the cutthroat race to map uncharted territories, or the quiet, behind-the-scenes moments that sparked massive revolutions, these books are deeply engaging and hard to put down.

NYT Best Seller

1776

David McCullough

In this masterful work of historical narrative, David McCullough tells the intensely human story of those who marched with General George Washington in the year of the Declaration of Independence. Drawing on extensive research, it vividly captures the immense challenges, devastating defeats, and unlikely victories of the American troops. It is a gripping, fast-paced account that makes the birth of a nation feel immediate and real.

Get the Book
My Recommendation

Hiroshima

John Hersey

This Pulitzer Prize-winning masterpiece tells the devastating, true stories of six survivors of the atomic bomb dropped on Hiroshima. Originally published in The New Yorker just a year after the attack, it humanized the staggering statistics and forced the world to confront the horrifying reality of nuclear warfare. It is a profoundly moving, unforgettable testament to human resilience in the face of unimaginable tragedy.

If you liked 'Chernobyl' (HBO)...

Midnight in Chernobyl

Adam Higginbotham

This gripping, meticulously researched account of the 1986 nuclear disaster strips away decades of Soviet propaganda to reveal the unvarnished truth. It focuses on the unbelievable stories of the plant workers, firefighters, and scientists who sacrificed everything to stop the meltdown. It is a terrifying, cinematic, and profoundly moving historical masterpiece.

Self-Help Books

Sometimes you don't just want to read about the world—you want to actively improve your place within it. The self-help genre is a goldmine of psychological insights, practical productivity tips, and profound advice on how to live a happier, more intentional life. It is like having a mentor, a therapist, and a life coach all bound up in a single paperback.

The best self-help books don't just offer empty platitudes; they provide deeply researched, actionable steps that you can apply to your daily routine immediately. Whether you are trying to break a bad habit, find the courage to pursue a creative passion, or learn how to communicate better with the people you love, there is a book in this section ready to guide you.

NYT Best Seller

Atomic Habits

James Clear

No matter your goals, this book offers a proven framework for improving every single day. James Clear reveals incredibly practical strategies that will teach you exactly how to form good habits, break bad ones, and master the tiny behaviors that lead to remarkable results. It is the ultimate productivity manual for anyone looking to optimize their life.

My Recommendation

The Art of Letting Go

Nick Trenton

If you constantly find yourself overthinking, holding onto past hurts, or battling anxiety, this book offers a compassionate roadmap to finding peace. Nick Trenton provides practical, actionable strategies to break free from negative thought spirals and release the emotional baggage weighing you down. It is a gentle, straightforward guide to rewiring your brain and finally living in the present moment.

Get the Book
If you liked 'Inside Out'...

Atlas of the Heart

Brené Brown

If you want to deeply understand human emotions and how we connect with one another, this is a phenomenal read. It maps out eighty-seven different emotions and experiences, giving you the vocabulary to truly understand yourself and the people around you. It is a powerful roadmap for building meaningful connections and living a courageous life.
